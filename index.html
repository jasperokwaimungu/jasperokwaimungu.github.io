About

I am a Ph.D. Candidate in Economics at Osaka University, where I also earned my M.A. in Economics.

My research lies at the intersection of international trade, development economics, and public finance. I study how trade policy, macroeconomic shocks, and public institutions influence economic development in emerging economies. My work combines rigorous empirical analysis with policy-relevant questions to better understand the drivers of sustainable and inclusive growth.

Research

Research Interests

My research interests lie at the intersection of international trade, development economics, and public finance. I am particularly interested in how trade policy, fiscal policy, macroeconomic conditions, and public institutions shape firm behavior, export performance, and welfare outcomes in emerging economies.

International Trade Development Economics Public Finance Trade Policy Fiscal Policy Macroeconomic Shocks Export Performance Emerging Economies

Research Agenda

Broadly, my work examines how developing economies respond to policy and macroeconomic changes. I am interested in the channels through which trade and fiscal policies affect production decisions, external competitiveness, and inclusive economic development.

My research aims to generate empirical evidence that can inform policy debates on trade, taxation, public institutions, and development strategy.

Methods and Data Interests

I am interested in applied empirical research using product-level, firm-level, and country-level data. My methodological interests include panel data methods, causal inference, local projections, and applied econometric approaches for studying trade and development outcomes.
