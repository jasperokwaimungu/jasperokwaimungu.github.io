About

I am a PhD Candidate in Economics at Osaka University, where I also completed my master's degree. My research interests lie at the intersection of international trade, development economics, and public finance.

I am especially interested in how trade policy, macroeconomic shocks, and public institutions shape development outcomes in emerging economies.

Research

Trade Policy, Macroeconomic Shocks, and Export Performance

My current research examines how Nigeria’s 2014–2016 macro-financial crisis affected non-oil export performance, with a focus on whether tariff exposure through imported intermediate inputs shaped export adjustment.
