About

I am a Ph.D. Candidate in Economics at The University of Osaka, where I previously completed my M.A. in Economics.

My research interests span international trade, development economics, and public finance, with a particular focus on emerging economies. I study how trade policy, macroeconomic shocks, and the quality of public institutions shape economic development, firm performance, and welfare. My research seeks to provide empirical evidence that informs policies promoting sustainable and inclusive economic growth.

Research

Trade Policy, Tax Policy, Macroeconomic Shocks, and Export Performance

I am currently investigating how macroeconomic shocks and trade policy interact to influence export performance in emerging economies, with a focus on firm-level adjustment and international trade.
